Siddhārtha Gautama

Religious leader whose teachings founded Buddhism
A statue of the Buddha from Sarnath, Uttar Pradesh, India, circa 475 CE
Here's a time line of Buddha's life
    563 BC - Born a prince in Lumbini, near the Nepalese-Indian border.
    563-547 BC - During his childhood he lives in his father's palace in Southern Nepal
    547 BC - At 16 he marries his cousin Princess Yasodhara
    547-533 BC - Spends his twenties living a lavish royal lifestyle. He has three palaces one for the hot season, one for the cold season and one for the rainy season. He has a son in his late twenties
    533 BC - He leaves the palace and sees an old person, a sick person and a dead person. For the first time he realizes there is old age, sickness and death and people have little control over their lives. He also sees a holy man which inspires him to change his life
    533 BC - On his 29th birthday he gives secretly up his royal life and leaves the palace while everyone is sleeping
    533 - 528 BC - He travels homeless and poor studying under spiritual and religious leaders. He leaves traditional religion and searches for truth through meditation
    528 BC - He has the Great Enlightment while meditating under a tree in Bihar, India. He spends the next 7 weeks meditating in the same area and at 35 becomes a Buddha or a person who has attained full enlightenment
    528 BC - He gives his first sermon to what became the first five members of the Buddhist order. This is the beginning of Buddhist teachings
    528 - 527 BC - Thousands convert to Buddhism and hear sermons delivered either by Buddha himself or monks of his order
    527 BC - He returns to his father's palace to convert the royal family to Buddhism
    523 - Despite protests his aunt is the first woman to become a monk and from then on women were allowed to join the order
    523 - 483 BC - He travels around Northern India teaching Buddhism and hundreds of thousands of people convert and follow his teachings
    483 - At 80 years old he passes away from food poisoning in a forest in Nepal
“No one saves us but ourselves. No one can and no one may. We ourselves must walk the path.” -- Gautama Buddha
